# Common Mistakes in Transformer Maintenance

This section highlights common mistakes observed during transformer maintenance and inspection, especially by beginners.

## 1. Ignoring Oil Level Checks
- Not checking oil level regularly  
- Operating transformer with low oil  

**Impact:** Overheating and insulation failure  

---

## 2. Skipping Safety Procedures
- Not using PPE  
- Touching components without proper isolation  

**Impact:** Serious injury or electrical hazards  

---

## 3. Poor Inspection Routine
- Irregular daily checks  
- Missing early warning signs like noise or vibration  

**Impact:** Small issues become major failures  

---

## 4. Improper Troubleshooting
- Guess-based fixes instead of step-by-step checking  
- Not identifying root cause  

**Impact:** Repeated failures and downtime  

---

## 5. Ignoring Temperature Monitoring
- Not tracking temperature rise  
- Overloading without checking limits  

**Impact:** Damage to windings and reduced lifespan  

---

## 6. Incomplete Reporting
- Not documenting observations  
- Missing important details in reports  

**Impact:** Difficult to track issues over time  

---

## Best Practice

Always follow a structured approach:
1. Inspect regularly  
2. Follow safety protocols  
3. Document everything  
4. Act early on abnormalities
